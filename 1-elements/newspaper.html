For First Time, Tests With Gene Therapy Begin

Jan. 30, 1991

Two cancer patients yesterday became the first people to receive
gene therapy in an attempt to defeat otherwise incurable tumors.

In a simple procedure that lasted barely 20 minutes, the patients, a
29-year-old woman and a 42-year-old man, were each infused with
about 100 million white blood cells. The cells enfolded a genetically
engineered copy of a gene that makes a potent anti-cancer enzyme.
